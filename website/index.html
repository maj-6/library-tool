About Library Downloads

A workbench for rare botanical works

Library Tool catalogues old and rare books on botany and herbal medicine — reconciling a private collection against the World Herb Library, tracking copyright, and turning scans into readable, searchable volumes.

What it does

It checks a book against the catalogue, hunts for a scan on the Internet Archive and HathiTrust, and works out whether the work is in copyright — reading the original registration and its renewal, and showing the records behind the verdict rather than a bare answer.

Scans that carry a text layer can be read beside the page image, with the text laid out where it sits on the paper. Scans that do not can be put through OCR, page by page. A companion Android app photographs title pages by voice, so a shelf can be catalogued without touching the phone.

The library

Finished volumes are published here. Everything in the library is a public-domain scan, browsable and downloadable in full. This is an experiment: it is the explorer that will eventually live on worldherblibrary.org.

Get it

The desktop app runs on Windows; the capture app runs on Android. Both are on the downloads page.

Built by Andrew Miller. The cataloguing tool is a local application — your books, notes and API keys stay on your own machine unless you publish them.
